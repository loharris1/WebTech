This page will provide you with a full scope of Jerusalem. This includes: the city background, where to find the best food, and its famous sites. After reading this page, you will be ready to visit Jerusalem!

City Background

Jerusalem is the capital of Israel. It is a young, hip city with a lot of history - frequently referred to as The Holy City. There are many things to do in this popular destination. Tayelet, which is categorized as a boardwalk, is an incredibly beautiful feature of Tel Aviv. You could spend the day strong up and down the lively street of music, restaurants, and vendors to then make your way to the beach – soaking up the sun. Neve Tzedek is a popular neighborhood in Jerusalem that is best suited for a younger, student demographic. The streets have a lot of artistic sculptures and street art.

Best Food

The best place to grab some grub is Carmel Market. The market has been around for almost 100 years and continues to bring the entire community together. Along the market, you can grab souvenirs, a snack or smoothie of fresh fruit, or breads & pastry. This market has everything one could need and is filled with friendly tourists and vendors. When you are there, you have to try vendor’s samples of dried fruit and venture into spice shops for a sniff! Pro Tip: practice Hebrew sentences to barter with vendors!

Famous Sites

The Dome of the Rock is one of Israel’s most recognizable monuments. It is an Islamic shrine located on the Temple Mount in Jerusalem. Also, incredibly recognizable is the Western Wall. It is a place of prayer and has two sections: one for women and one for men. Cracks of the bricks of the wall contain prayers and letters to an individual’s higher power. The Wall holds a special memory for me and my Israeli family.
