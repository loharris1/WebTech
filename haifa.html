This page will provide you with a full scope for visiting the city of Haifa. This includes information on: its Baha'i Gardens, information on the park's cable cars, and its tasty Fattoush Restaurant. After reading this page, you will be ready to visit Haifa!

Baha'i Gardens

Haifa’s number one tourist attraction is the Baha’i Gardens. Many call it Israel’s horticultural wonder. A tour guide has explained to us that the size of it is roughly more than 1,000 tennis courts and took 70 years and 250 million dollars to build it. The structure also required tons of water to keep plants hydrated and healthy. Today, you can find a lot of students there taking artsy photos among the flowers and photographers capturing the beauty of the garden.

Cable Cars

An activity for all ages, if they’re not afraid of heights, is to ride the Haifa cable car. The car drops you off at the Stella Maris Carmelite Monastery. The ride is absolutely beautiful as one can see the entirety of the city extending to the Mediterranean Sea. Once you take the cable upwards, you can get an even better view from the monastery. The monastery has some of the most beautiful paintings and glass art. The cable cars run from 9am – 8pm and only cost 28 INS (Israeli New Shekels) for a round-trip.

Fattoush Restaurant

If you go to Haifa, you have to try Fattoush, Not the salad, but the best restaurant in the city! The place is known for its American-size portions and Arabic coffee. Their website features aspects of its location in photographs and a beautifully shot promotionally video. From kunefeh, to hummus, to shakshuka – this place serves up some tasty plates! They serve family style, so go in a large group to get an incredible deal.
