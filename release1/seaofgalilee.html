Location Background

The Sea of Galilee is known as the Lake of Jesus. It is one of the most sacred places and has many names. One name is Kinneret, which in Hebrew means violin, because the sea is in the shape of a violin. The Sea of Galilee is located in north-east Israel near the borders with Jordan and Syria. It is the largest freshwater lake in Israel and its size can be comparable to Washington D.C. There are ancient hot springs near the sea, which many view as sacred and healing. The body of water is most famous for being where Jesus walked on water.

Living in the City

If you love the area, you may want to stay within the city walls. There are many hotels and each with very interesting stories. For example, The Scots Hotel was formally a 19th-century Scottish hospital. Now, it’s fully furnished with spas and huge rooms! Also, you may try a bed-and-breakfast like Villa Roca Tiberias. This was a house for the ill reverted into a cozy, family friendly hotel. The Sea of Galilee area is also known for its hostel living options. Our additional site created for this area has more information about hostels in this region.

Decks Resturant

If you are in the region of the Sea of Galilee, Decks Resturant is a must. Decks Resturant is an incredible spot for carnivores like myself! Wow. The restaurant is known for its steaks, seafood, and staple Mediterranean cuisine. Lemon, parsley fries are a must and are the center of family platters or side dishes.
