This page will provide you with a full scope for visiting Masada. This includes: its background, information on the park's light shows, and the famous Taj Maahal Restaurant. After reading this page, you will be ready to visit Masada National Park!

Park Background

Masada is a beautiful national park in Israel. Many call it an ancient fortress within Israel’s Judean Desert. It is the greatest archaeological sites in Israel and was built around 30 BCE. Many choose to climb the winding “snake path” to get to the top of Masada. Or, you can be like myself, and take the cable car and still get in some moderate steps. It is common to climb Masada at sunrise to get a beautiful view of the Dead Sea and the Moab Mountains.

Light Shows

An insider tip you may have not known about is the unique light and sound shows that occur within Masada. These shows are concerts that present against the western side wall of Masada. During the summer months in Israel, you can catch one of these shows that usually depict historical events that took place right where you come to visit. Taj Maahal Resturant

Before you catch a sound and light show, you may want to a local restaurant to get some food. This is especially true for those who burned up their calories climbing the “snake path”! What better a place than Taj Maahal – an Israeli restaurant with such cool features. Not only is the food amazing, sharable, and incredibly unique – there are so many aspects to the restaurant that make it a must. First, there are lounge chairs for you to eat at. Many patrons choose to sit crisscross, on the floor set with Israeli cushions, to enjoy their meals. The ambiance is incredible and there is even live performance! Belly dancers will strut up and down rows to entertain guests. This restaurant is one of my favorites because it serves great food and a show!
