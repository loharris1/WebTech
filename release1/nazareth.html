Nazareth is an amazing city and this page will tell you why! The page includes information on Nazareth's background, popular Nazareth Village, and Tishreen Restaurant. After reading this page, you will be ready to visit historical Nazareth.

City Background

Nazareth is the city in which Jesus is said to have grown up. During the time of Jesus, the city was predominately Jewish, after, Nazareth became Christian center! On a map, it is located within the lower Galilee region in the north of Israel. While my time there, I learned it was the #1 largest Arab city in Israel! Today, a majority of residents are Muslim.

Nazareth Village

In Nazareth, the attraction of the Nazareth Village allows you to reenacts village life in the Galilee time of Jesus. When I visited, I was met with a large group of people dressed in first-century clothing. The village had many facets: an olive press, stone quarries, grape vines, and a first-century synagogue. The tour we took, which was standard for the village, was around an hour and ended with a meal! This activity is well-suited for families – as it can be a fun bonding experience for a small group!

Tishreen Resturant

During my time in Nazareth, I can say the best restaurant is Tishreen. This spot is located right on the main street of El-Bishara and is known for its signature salads. For instance, my family ordered the salad pictured above, their most popular, and it was absolutely incredible. Their Shanklish Salad, featuring tomatoes, mint, onion, lemon & olive, and shanklish cheese, makes it easy for someone to go on a Mediterranean diet! It is the perfect refreshing treat after a hot day strolling around Nazareth’s cobblestone streets.
